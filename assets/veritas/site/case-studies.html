The challenge
Invisible online.
Relying entirely on word of mouth.

San Miguel Painting Co. was doing quality work — commercial and residential jobs across North Texas — but operating entirely through referrals and personal networks. No website. No digital presence. No way for a prospect to find them, verify them, or contact them outside of a direct introduction.

The owner was the sales team. Every new job came in through a phone call or a personal connection. When the phone didn't ring, the pipeline was empty. When a prospect searched online — which they all do, even after a referral — there was nothing there to confirm the business was real, professional, or worth calling back.

The result: leads that could have been easy wins were dying in the gap between referral and first contact. A painting company that should have been converting at a high rate was losing business to competitors with nothing more than a basic website and a Google profile.

The solution
A professional digital presence that works while he works.

Veritas AI Partners deployed the TradeOS Digital Presence service line: a branded, professional website with automated lead capture built from the ground up in under 30 days.

The build started with brand positioning — who San Miguel serves, what sets their work apart, and what a commercial or residential client needs to see before they pick up the phone. The site was then built to answer those questions instantly: professional photography-ready layout, clear service descriptions, testimonial structure, and a frictionless estimate request form that feeds directly into a lead intake pipeline.

The intake system captures name, contact, project type, location, and scope — routing each submission as a structured lead record the owner can act on immediately, from any device, without needing to manage email manually.

The site was deployed on Netlify with a custom domain configuration, mobile-optimized for the way North Texas contractors and property managers actually browse, and built to load fast on any connection.
